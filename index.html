About

I'm a Berklee College of Music student majoring in Music Business. I'm interested in how artists build audiences across cultures and how new platforms can improve the way fans support creators. Right now I'm focused on strengthening my marketing strategy skills and learning how to think like a product builder.

This semester I'm taking Music Product, Music & Web3, and entrepreneurship classes. My biggest takeaway isn't just new information. It's a change in how I think. I'm paying closer attention to what's actually happening in the music business right now, and where I can realistically fit as a Berklee business student in the AI era.

I'm especially interested in the intersection of music and new technology, including AI, platforms, and blockchain. But I only care about it when it connects to real problems and real incentives, not hype.

The questions I keep coming back to are rights and royalty transparency, how platform algorithms shape discovery, and how everyday artists can build sustainable careers instead of depending on luck. I'm also interested in how these issues play out across different markets, especially the U.S. and China, because the business rules, culture, and regulation can look completely different.

More than anything, I'm here because I genuinely love this industry. I want artists who work hard and have real talent to be seen, treated fairly, and given a real chance.

What I'm Building

This semester, I'm prototyping a small music-tech idea in my Music & Web3 class, starting from a real rights/royalty problem and turning it into a simple user flow.

It's for independent artists who want clearer visibility into what's happening with their music and how licensing choices connect to earnings.

It solves one problem: making rights and payout systems easier to understand and act on, with less guesswork and fewer hidden steps.
