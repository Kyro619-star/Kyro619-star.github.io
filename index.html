About

I'm a Berklee College of Music student majoring in Music Business. I'm interested in how artists build audiences across cultures and how new platforms can improve the way fans support creators. Right now I'm focused on strengthening my marketing strategy skills and learning how to think like a product builder.

This semester I'm taking Music Product, Music & Web3, and entrepreneurship classes, and my biggest takeaway isn't just new knowledge — it's a shift in how I think. I'm paying closer attention to what's actually happening in the music business right now, and where I can realistically fit as a Berklee business student in the AI era. I'm especially curious about the intersection of music and new technology (AI, platforms, and blockchain…), but only when it's grounded in real problems and real incentives — not hype. The questions I keep coming back to are rights and royalty transparency, how platform algorithms shape discovery, and how everyday artists can build sustainable careers instead of relying on luck. I also care a lot about these issues across different markets, especially the U.S. and China, because the rules, culture, and regulation can be completely different. More than anything, I'm here because I genuinely love this industry — and I want to help artists who work hard and have real talent actually get seen, get treated fairly, and have a real chance.
